∎ RPI Zero Floor means that if the retail prices index is increasing, the income will increase accordingly, however if RPI goes negative the income will stay level until RPI increases again.

∎ RPI No Floor means that the income will do whatever RPI does, and if RPI goes negative, the client’s income will fall in line with this.

∎ LPI 2.5%: RPI with a cap at 2.5% (i.e. that the rate of RPI would be applied unless it exceeded 2.5%).

∎ LPI 5%: RPI with a cap at 5% (i.e. that the rate of RPI would be applied unless it exceeded 5%).

∎ Please note: Both forms of LPI carry a “zero floor” so will stay level if RPI ever goes negative.

• Standard Life RPI is based on ‘Zero Floor’

• Aviva is based on ‘Zero Floor’

• Legal & General is based on ‘Zero Floor’

• Scottish Widows is based on ‘No Floor’

• Canada Life is based on ‘No Floor’
